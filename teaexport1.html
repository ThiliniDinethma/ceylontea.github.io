Sri Lanka is one of Asia's developed countries. Tourism, export of tea, clothes, textiles, rice processing, and other agricultural products are the main doors of income to Sri Lanka. Our country has recently been one of the countries that sell fair trade tea to the United Kingdom and other countries. Tea is one of common people's most important beverages. And regular demand for tea products is on the rise. The industry includes a number of various tea items. Tea industry in Sri Lanka is one of the major sector in which the country gets foreign currency and is also the main drink of ordinary Sri Lankans. The demand for tea goods are growing in the world market. Since the world has a mixed economy and the economic system plays a key role in the country's development, tea brings a large income to the country.

Why ceylon tea has a hight demand?
Ceylon tea is a household name and a brand name for premium tea worldwide. The demand for Ceylon Tea persisted until the island became the biggest exporter of tea, even after independence in 1970.Ceylon tea is known for its high consistency and unrivalled flavour. In 2017, Ceylon tea was marked as the safest market tea in comparison to pesticides and other chemicals commonly contained in tea, the EU Surveillance Study. ISO named Ceylon tea the safest as far as pesticide residues were concerned in 1997 and 1999. The island was also the first in Montreal to gain the status of Ozone Tolerant Tea.
